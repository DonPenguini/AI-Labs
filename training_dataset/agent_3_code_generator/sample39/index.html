About Hohmann Transfer

The Hohmann transfer is the most fuel-efficient way to move between two circular coplanar orbits. It requires two engine burns: one to enter the elliptical transfer orbit, and another to circularize at the destination.

Astrodynamics Concepts

Coplanar Orbits

Both initial and final orbits lie in the same plane, eliminating the need for plane-change maneuvers which are extremely costly in terms of delta-v.

Impulsive Maneuvers

The Hohmann transfer assumes instantaneous velocity changes (impulses). Real burns take time but this approximation is valid for high thrust-to-weight ratios.

Energy Efficiency

The Hohmann transfer is optimal for transferring between circular orbits when the radius ratio is less than about 15.6. For larger ratios, bi-elliptic transfers become more efficient.
